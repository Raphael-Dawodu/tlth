Privacy Policy

Effective date: 16 April 2026
Last updated: 16 April 2026

At The Learning Treehouse, we care about your privacy and about handling your family's information with care, respect and transparency.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect personal information when you visit our website, contact us, enquire about our services, book support for your child, or use any of our forms, assessments or learning tools.

We have written this policy in clear, parent-friendly language. The ICO says privacy information should be easy to understand, accessible, and transparent.

1. Who we are

The Learning Treehouse Ltd is the data controller responsible for the personal information covered by this policy. Under UK data protection law, the data controller is the organisation that decides how and why personal information is used.

2. The information we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • parent or carer name;
  • child's name;
  • child's age or school year;
  • email address and telephone number;
  • information about the services you are interested in, such as Maths, English, Chess, tutoring, assessments or workshops;
  • booking, attendance and scheduling information;
  • information you share about your child's learning needs, progress, strengths, confidence or areas where support may be helpful;
  • responses submitted through forms, assessments or online learning tools;
  • payment and invoicing information, where relevant;
  • messages or correspondence you send to us; and
  • limited technical website information, such as device or browser data, where this is collected through website tools or cookies.

3. How we collect information

We may collect information:

  • when you contact us through the website;
  • when you email, call or message us;
  • when you book a consultation or register your interest;
  • when you complete a form, assessment or questionnaire;
  • when you enrol your child in a programme;
  • when you make a payment; and
  • automatically through cookies or similar website technologies, where these are used.

4. How we use personal information

We use personal information to:

  • respond to enquiries;
  • discuss the most suitable support for your child;
  • arrange consultations, assessments or bookings;
  • provide tutoring, learning support, clubs and related services;
  • communicate with parents and carers about sessions, progress and next steps;
  • keep service and attendance records;
  • send confirmations, reminders and follow-up communications;
  • manage payments and basic business administration;
  • improve our services and learning experience; and
  • send relevant updates or marketing communications where we are allowed to do so.

5. Our lawful bases

Under the UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing personal information. The lawful bases we are most likely to rely on are:

Contract
Where processing is necessary to take steps at your request before providing services, or to deliver tutoring, assessments, bookings or related support you have asked us to provide.

Legitimate interests
Where processing is reasonably necessary for running and improving The Learning Treehouse, communicating with families, keeping records, and delivering a safe, effective and well-organised service, provided this does not override your rights and freedoms.

Consent
Where we rely on consent, such as for certain marketing communications or non-essential cookies, you can withdraw that consent at any time.

Legal obligation
Where we need to keep or use information to comply with legal, tax, accounting, regulatory or safeguarding-related responsibilities.

6. Children's information

Our services are designed to support children, but our website, bookings and service arrangements are intended to be handled by a parent or carer wherever appropriate.

Because children's information deserves particular care, we aim to collect only the information that is reasonably needed to support learning, provide services, communicate with families and maintain appropriate records.

7. Marketing communications

If you contact us or register your interest, we may follow up about your enquiry and about suitable next steps for your child.

Where the law requires consent for marketing, we will ask for it. If we send marketing based on consent, you can withdraw that consent at any time by contacting us or using any unsubscribe option provided.

8. Who we may share information with

We do not sell your personal information.

We may share information, where necessary, with trusted service providers who help us run our business, such as providers of:

  • website hosting;
  • email;
  • cloud storage;
  • online forms or spreadsheets;
  • booking and scheduling tools;
  • payment processing; and
  • professional business support services.

We may also share information where required by law, where necessary to protect legal rights, or where safeguarding concerns make this necessary and lawful.

9. International transfers

Some service providers may process or store information outside the UK. If that happens, we will take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place so that personal information remains protected in line with UK data protection requirements.

10. How long we keep information

We keep personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, including for service delivery, record keeping, legal, accounting and administrative purposes.

As a general guide:

  • general enquiries may be kept for up to 12 months after the last meaningful contact;
  • learner and parent records may be kept for the duration of the learning relationship and for a reasonable period afterwards;
  • financial records may be kept for as long as required by law; and
  • assessment or progress records may be kept for as long as reasonably necessary to support the child's learning journey and maintain service records.

11. Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you may have the right to:

  • ask for access to your personal information;
  • ask for inaccurate information to be corrected;
  • ask for information to be erased in certain circumstances;
  • ask for processing to be restricted in certain circumstances;
  • object to certain types of processing;
  • ask for data portability in certain circumstances; and
  • withdraw consent at any time where consent is the lawful basis.

These rights are not absolute, but we will respond to requests in line with applicable law.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact: contactus@thelearningtreehouse.co.uk

12. Data security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to help protect personal information from unauthorised access, misuse, loss, alteration or disclosure. However, no online service can ever be completely secure.

13. Third-party links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites or services. If you follow a link to another website or platform, that provider will have its own privacy and cookie practices.

14. How to complain

If you have any concerns about how we use personal information, please contact us first and we will do our best to help.

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), which is the UK regulator for data protection matters.

15. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes to our services, website, tools, legal requirements or business practices. The most recent version will always be published on this page.
